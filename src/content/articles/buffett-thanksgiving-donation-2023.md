# Berkshire Hathaway News Release (November 21, 2023)

**Author:** Warren E. Buffett
**Published:** November 21, 2023
**Source:** [Berkshire Hathaway](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/news/nov2123.pdf)

## Overview

Warren Buffett announced the conversion of 1,600 Berkshire A shares into 2,400,000 B shares, donated to four family foundations. This repeated his Thanksgiving 2022 donation and was part of his 2006 lifetime pledge commitment.

## The Donation

| Foundation | Shares Received |
|------------|----------------|
| Susan Thompson Buffett Foundation | 1,500,000 |
| Sherwood Foundation | 300,000 |
| Howard G. Buffett Foundation | 300,000 |
| NoVo Foundation | 300,000 |

## Key Themes

### On Dynastic Wealth

> "My three children, along with their father, have a common belief that dynastic wealth, though both legal and common in much of the world including the United States, is not desirable."

### The Ovarian Lottery

> "The United States is exhibit A for that belief, and the four of us feel lucky that we beat very long odds when we were born in the U.S."

### On Capitalism

> "We also agree that capitalism – whatever its weaknesses, including the vast disparities in wealth and political influence that it delivers somewhat capriciously to its citizens – has worked wonders and continues to work wonders."

### On Wealth and Wisdom

> "We have had many opportunities to observe that being rich does not make you either wise *or* evil."

## Succession Planning

Buffett addressed Berkshire's future post-death:

> "We have the right CEO to succeed me and the right Board of Directors as well. Both are needed."

> "Berkshire – one of the largest and most diversified companies in the world – will inevitably encounter human errors in judgment and behavior... But these mistakes are unlikely to be serious at Berkshire and will be acknowledged and corrected."

## The Testamentary Trust

Key provisions outlined:
- **Three children as unanimous trustees** — All decisions require consensus
- **Successors must be designated** — Due to mortality's random nature
- **Self-liquidating after ~10 years**
- **Lean staff operation**
- **Open book** — No hidden trusts or foreign entities; simple will available at Douglas County Courthouse

## Historical Context

Buffett at 93 reflected on his situation:

> "At 93, I feel good but fully realize I am playing in extra innings."

This followed his 2006 pledges, available at berkshirehathaway.com, which include conditions recipients must continue to meet.
