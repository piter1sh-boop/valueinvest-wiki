# Key Quotes from 1994 Shareholder Letter

1. > "A fat wallet is the enemy of superior investment results. And Berkshire now has a net worth of $11.9 billion compared to about $22 million when Charlie and I began."

2. > "My argument is, to be a good hitter, you've got to get a good ball to hit. It's the first rule in the book. If I have to bite at stuff that is out of my happy zone, I'm not a .344 hitter. I might only be a .250 hitter."

3. > "We will continue to ignore political and economic forecasts, which are an expensive distraction for many investors."

4. > "Fear is the foe of the faddist, but the friend of the fundamentalist."

5. > "We will not ask you to invest with us and then put our own money elsewhere."
