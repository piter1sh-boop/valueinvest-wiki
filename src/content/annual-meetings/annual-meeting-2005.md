# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 2005

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 2005

## Overview

- **Notable**: Poor Charlie's Almanack for sale; insurance industry investigation limits

## Key Topics

### Poor Charlie's Almanack

Buffett highly recommended Peter Kaufman's book:
> "Poor Charlie's Almanack is next door in the exhibition hall. And it's an absolutely terrific book that Peter Kaufman has put together."

> "It's Charlie at his best. And Charlie's at his best most of the time, but it's a real gem."

### What Buffett Won't Discuss

Three off-limits topics:
1. Last year's Nebraska football season
2. What Berkshire is buying or selling
3. Specific details about insurance industry investigation (to protect witness integrity)

On the investigation:
> "Witnesses are not supposed to talk to each other and, of course, if you talk — we don't do that."

### Insurance Catastrophe Risk

Buffett explained hurricane seasonality:
> "50 percent of the hurricanes, roughly, occur in September and about maybe 17 and-a-half percent in October and August, and the balance, maybe, in November and July."

### Gorat's Dinner

Buffett warned about sold-out reservations at Gorat's Steak House (the traditional post-meeting dinner):
> "Be sure not to go to Gorat's unless you have a reservation tomorrow, because they're sold out."

### Suze's Disneyland Line

Buffett shared family humor about daughter Suzie:
> "She looked at me and said, 'I waited in line at Disneyland.'"
