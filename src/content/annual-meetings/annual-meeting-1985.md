# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 1985

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | May 1985

## Overview

- **Attendance**: ~250 shareholders (compared to 30 in 1980)
- **Venue**: Red Lion Inn, downtown Omaha (later moved to Joslyn Art Museum)
- **Duration**: 2 hours

## Key Topics

### How to Value a Business

Buffett recommended:
- Reading Benjamin Graham and Philip Fisher for investment mindset
- Annual reports and trade magazines for evaluating specific businesses
- Reading is more important than talking to executives

> "You are not going to get any brilliant insights walking into the [Exxon] building."

### Dividend Policy

- Berkshire had not paid a dividend in nearly 20 years
- 88% of shareholders approved of reinvesting earnings rather than paying dividends
- Similar percentage approved of Berkshire's charitable contribution policy

### Inflation Outlook

Buffett predicted:
- 7% "moderate inflation" as most likely
- 5% chance of "runaway inflation"

### Post-Buffett Era

Buffett reassured shareholders:
> "If something happened to me tomorrow, Berkshire will not be sold and parts of it will not be sold."

Charlie Munger would step in as successor.

### Capital Allocation

Buffett preference:
> "We would really like a collection of whole businesses that meet our [investment] standards."

### Growth Expectations

Buffett cautioned:
> "I can guarantee [that] we will not do as well as in the past."

However, Berkshire outperformed: 15.9% annual return vs S&P 500's 11.1% since 1985.

## Famous Quotes

- On federal deficit: "I'll believe it when I see it."
- On junk bonds: "I think they'll live up to their name."
