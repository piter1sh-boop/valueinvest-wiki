# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 2008

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 2008

## Overview

- **Attendance**: ~31,000 people
- **Opening**: Susan Lucci (soap opera star) traded jobs with Buffett in a skit about dividends and guidance

## Key Topics

### How NOT to Be a Lemming

Buffett shared his transformation:
- Started investing at age 11
- Wandered with technical analysis for 8 years
- Read "The Intelligent Investor" by Ben Graham at age 19

> "If you absorb the lessons of 'The Intelligent Investor', mainly in Chapters 8 and 20, you will not behave like a lemming."

### Three Big Lessons from The Intelligent Investor

1. **Think of stocks as parts of a business**
2. **Use the market to serve you, not instruct you**
3. **Always leave a margin of safety**

### Farm Investment Analogy

> "If you were going to buy a farm a few miles outside of Omaha, you would not get a price on it every day."

When buying stocks, you should:
- Look at what the business will produce over time
- Evaluate expected yields, prices, taxes, costs
- Ignore daily quotes

### Forget About the Word "Stock"

> "When we buy a stock, we would be happy with that stock if they told us the market was going to close for a couple years. We look to the business."

### Cologne Re

Buffett explained that when Berkshire owns 100% of Cologne Re, he will take over the investment portfolio.

### On Market Direction

> "Charlie and I haven't the faintest idea where the stock market is going to go next week, next month, or next year."

## Famous Quotes

- "We never talk about [market direction]. It never comes up."
- "We don't think — what we see when we look at the stock market is we see thousands and thousands of companies priced every day, and we ignore 99.9 percent of what we see."
