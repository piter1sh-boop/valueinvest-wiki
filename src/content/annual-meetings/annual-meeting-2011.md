# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 2011

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 2011

## Overview

- **Theme**: Catastrophe losses and Lubrizol controversy
- **Notable**: David Sokol/Lubrizol situation discussed transparently

## Key Topics

### Lubrizol Situation

Buffett addressed the David Sokol controversy:
> "We're going to talk briefly about the David Sokol/Lubrizol situation, and then we're going to open it up for your questions. Anything as it relates to the Lubrizol matter is going to be transcribed and will be put up on the website."

Full transcript was posted to Berkshire website promptly.

### Asian Catastrophes

Q1 earnings severely impacted by global catastrophes:
> "In the first quarter of this year, we had some major catastrophes in the Pacific Asian areas, and that hit the reinsurance industry particularly hard."

- New Zealand earthquake: $12 billion insured damage
- Japanese earthquake and tsunami
- Total estimated loss: $1.673 billion pretax

> "Probably the second worst quarter for the insurance industry, in terms of catastrophes around the globe."

First time in nine years Berkshire likely to have an insurance underwriting loss.

### BNSF Railroad

Buffett bullish on railroads' competitive advantage:
> "The competitive advantage of railroads is becoming more and more evident, almost by the day, particularly as fuel prices increase."

BNSF had a "very significant gain in earnings" and looked to have a "very good year."

### GEICO Growth

GEICO continued strong growth—adding 318,000 policies by meeting time, up to 381,000 by year-end.
> "Every policyholder at GEICO, on average, has a value to us, of something on the order of $1,500."

### High-Yield Investments Retiring

Buffett noted losing high-yield investments:
- Goldman Sachs preferred (called in April 2011)
- General Electric preferred (to be called in October 2011)
- Swiss Re note at 12%

> "We have lost, or are losing, at least three very high-yield investments, which we cannot replace with similar investments at present."

$38 billion in cash at quarter end, earning "virtually nothing."
