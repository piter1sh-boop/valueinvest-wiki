# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 2023

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 2023

## Overview

- **Theme**: Post-pandemic economic normalization
- **Notable**: "King Charles" Munger, $504B GAAP net worth, $165B float

## Key Topics

### King Charles

> "When I woke up this morning, I realized that we had a competitive broadcast going out somewhere in the UK. And they were celebrating a King Charles, and we've got our own King Charles here today."

### Lower Earnings Ahead

Buffett warned:
> "I would say that in the general economy... perhaps the majority of our businesses will actually report lower earnings this year than last year."

> "The incredible period... is about extraordinary as I've seen a business since World War II, which poured out a lot of money to people who couldn't get goods."

That "extraordinary period" has ended. Some managers had "too much inventory on order."

### Investment Income Rising

Short-term Treasury yields improved dramatically:
> "Not that long ago we were getting four basis points, which is next to nothing on that $125 billion, which means we were getting $50 million a year. And now... we bought $3 billion of bills at 5-90. That's 5.92 bond equivalent yield."

> "We will have... producing $5 billion a year."

### Berkshire's Scale

> "We had on March 31st... $504 billion of GAAP net worth. Now, what might surprise you is that there's no other company in the United States that has a number that is that large."

### Float Explained

Ajit's insurance float reached $165 billion — up from "a pittance in 1986":
> "It's like having a bank with no employees, no interest, and no ability to withdraw the money in a hurry that we have working for us."

> "Practically all years, [float] hasn't cost us anything."

### Retaining Earnings

> "If we're retaining $30 billion or $35 billion, or whatever it may be, a year, they should expect more operating earnings over time."
