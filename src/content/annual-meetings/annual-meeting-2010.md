# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 2010

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 2010

## Overview

- **Theme**: Goldman Sachs SEC lawsuit and economic recovery
- **Notable**: Buffett vigorously defended Goldman Sachs against SEC charges

## Key Topics

### Goldman Sachs Defense

Buffett gave an extensive defense of Goldman Sachs following the SEC's lawsuit over the ABACUS transaction:
> "Goldman itself was a loser. They didn't intend to be a loser, I'm sure. They couldn't sell the piece — a piece of the transaction — and they kept it, and I think they lost 90 or $100 million because they kept it."

On ABN AMRO's role:
> "They took on a $900 million risk of guaranteeing credit. They got paid about a million-six. And the company whose credit they guaranteed went broke, and so they had to pay the 900 million. It's a little hard for me to get terribly sympathetic with the fact that a bank made a dumb credit deal."

### Focus on Operating Earnings

Buffett emphasized ignoring GAAP accounting fluctuations:
> "We don't really think they mean anything on a quarterly basis. Obviously, they're meaningful over the years... And in any quarter, they mean absolutely nothing."

On earnings per share games:
> "If I had told our managers that we would earn three dollars and 17 1/2 cents for the quarter, you know, they might do a little fudging in order to make sure that we actually came out at that number."

### Economic Recovery

Berkshire saw improvement in March and April 2010:
> "What was very spotty in the recovery a couple of months ago, the trends really seem a fair amount stronger in the last few months."

Railroad, Marmon, and ISCAR showed "pretty good uptick."

### Board Introductions

Directors: Howard Buffett, Steve Burke, Susan Decker, Bill Gates, Sandy Gottesman, Charlotte Guyman, Don Keough (recovering from operation), Tom Murphy, Ron Olson, Walter Scott.
