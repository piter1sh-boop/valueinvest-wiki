# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 1998

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 1998

## Overview

- **Attendance**: Overflow rooms needed
- **Format**: Business meeting + Q&A until 3:30 PM with noon break

## Key Topics

### Price/Earnings Ratios

Buffett explained that P/E ratios move up because:
- People expect industry/company prospects to be better relative to other securities
- Decreasing interest rates have been pushing stocks up
- Corporate profits and return on equity have improved

### Olympic Diving Analogy

> "You get paid just as well for the most simple dive, as long as you execute it all right."

Buffett contrasted investing with Olympic diving, which has a "degree of difficulty" factor. In investing, there's no bonus for difficulty.

### Efficient Market Hypothesis

Buffett criticized EMH's influence on business schools:
> "It amazes me... I think to some extent [EMH] has contaminated the teaching of investing in the universities."

Munger added: "The contamination was massive. But it's waning."

### Finding a Mentor

Buffett recommended:
- University of Florida (Mason Hawkins funded courses)
- Columbia University (practitioners visit to teach)

### Shareholder Letters

Buffett advised reading shareholder letters from multiple companies to learn about businesses.

## Famous Quotes

> "We look for one-foot bars to step over rather than seven-foot or eight-foot bars."
