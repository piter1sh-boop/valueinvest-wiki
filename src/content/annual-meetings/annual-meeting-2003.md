# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 2003

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 2003

## Overview

- **Theme**: Celebrating individual shareholders
- **Notable**: Andy Heyward's "Liberty's Kids" educational cartoons

## Key Topics

### Real Shareholders

Buffett celebrated Berkshire's shareholder base:
> "One of the things that makes it fun to run Berkshire is that we see real shareholders. We probably have a larger proportion of our shares held by individuals than virtually any large company in America."

### Munger's Wisdom Calibration

Buffett shared how to interpret Munger's responses:

> "You have to learn to calibrate Charlie's answers. When I ask him whether he likes something, if he says 'No,' that means we put all our money in it. If he says, 'That's the dumbest idea I've ever heard,' that's a more moderate investment."

### Board Composition

Berkshire planned to add directors who meet four tests:
1. Business savvy
2. Personal stake (own money in Berkshire)
3. Interest in the company
4. Shareholder orientation

### New Directors

New directors would have "a lot of their own money on the line, just like you do."
