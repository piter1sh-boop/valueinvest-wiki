# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 1997

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 1997

## Overview

- **Attendance**: All 50 US states represented
- **Notable**: Buffett losing his voice, international visitors from many countries

## Key Topics

### Losing His Voice

Buffett opened with humor:
> "I had a real problem last night. I was losing my voice almost entirely. I don't want you to think I lost it cheering for myself this morning."

He promised various non-Coca-Cola products to keep his voice going.

### Zone Microphone System

12 microphones placed around the venue for audience questions.

### International Attendance

> "All 50 states — at least in terms of tickets — all 50 states are represented."

Visitors from: South Africa, Australia, Brazil, England, France, Germany, Greece, Hong Kong, Ireland, Iceland, Israel, Saipan, New Zealand, Saudi Arabia, Singapore, Sweden, Switzerland.

Buffett joked about Nebraska attendance:
> "We have an increased percentage from last year who come from Nebraska this year. And you have to be a little careful in interpreting that, because some people say they're from Nebraska and really aren't, because for status reasons."

### Directors

Howard Buffett, Susan Buffett, Walter Scott, Malcolm Chace ("Kim") — described as "a rather low-paying board."
