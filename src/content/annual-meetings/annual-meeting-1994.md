# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 1994

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 1994

## Overview

- **Attendance**: ~3,300 in main room, grew ~600 from previous year
- **Venue**: Moved from Joslyn Museum to an old vaudeville theater
- **Notable**: Munger won "Miss Congeniality" — only director candidate with no negative votes

## Key Topics

### Derivatives Warning

Buffett issued an early warning on derivatives:
> "Excessive risks in the reinsurance business may not become evident until big problems arise years later."

He called derivatives a dangerous combination of "ignorance and borrowed money."

### Cultural Slide

Buffett joked about the venue change:
> "We were holding this meeting at the Joslyn Museum, which is a temple of culture. And we've now, of course, moved to an old vaudeville theater. The only place in town that can hold us next year is the Ak-Sar-Ben Coliseum where they have keno and racetracks."

> "We are sliding down the cultural chain — just as Charlie predicted years ago."

### Munger's Miss Congeniality Award

> "When you lose out the title of Miss Congeniality to Charlie, you know you're in trouble."

### Meeting Format

- Business meeting first
- Q&A until noon
- Optional continuation until ~1:15 PM
- Buses available to hotels, airports, and Berkshire commercial establishments

### Attendance

Shareholders from 49 of 50 US states (only Vermont absent), plus international visitors from Australia, Israel, Sweden, France, UK, Canada, and more.
