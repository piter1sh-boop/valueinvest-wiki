# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 2004

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 2004

## Overview

- **Notable**: Four questions for auditors introduced; strict one-question rule

## Key Topics

### Auditor Vigilance

Buffett advocated for stronger auditor questioning:
> "For many years, particularly in the '90s, I think there was a weakening, frankly, in auditor vigilance. And the trick is really to have the auditors more worried about the audit committee than they are worried about the management."

> "If such procedures had been followed over the years, there would have been a lot less trouble in corporate America."

Buffett announced Berkshire's audit committee asks four specific questions to Deloitte & Touche annually.

### Directors Introduced

Susan T. Buffett, Howard G. Buffett, Malcolm G. Chace, David S. Gottesman (Sandy had conflict — granddaughter's bat mitzvah), Charlotte Guyman, Donald R. Keough, Thomas S. Murphy, Ronald L. Olson, Walter Scott Jr.

### One Question Rule

After complaints about multi-part questions:
> "The bigger reason is Charlie and I can't remember the first part by the time you get to the fifth part."

> "Only one question at a time and we will go around from microphone to microphone."

### No Recording

> "Any audio or video recording of this meeting is prohibited."

### Liberty Kids

Andy Heyward's animated series was highlighted as "great for kids but great for adults too."
