# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 2009

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 2009

## Overview

- **Theme**: Financial crisis aftermath and recovery
- **Notable**: Record attendance with overflow rooms, journalist panel format

## Key Topics

### Treasury Bills vs Mattress

Buffett opened with a memorable joke about negative Treasury yields:
> "If the U.S. Treasury had just sold 5 trillion of these, they could've made an easy $90 million and Tim Geithner could've put the money under a Nervous Nellie mattress and we all would've been better off."

In December 2008, Berkshire sold $5 million in Treasury bills due April 2009 for $5 million and ninety dollars—meaning investors who bought them lost $90 compared to keeping cash under a mattress.

### Derivatives as Weapons of Mass Destruction

Buffett defended Berkshire's derivatives positions:
- $4.9 billion received for equity put options
- Collateral posting requirements were minimal (less than 1% of total marketable securities as of March 31, 2009)
- The real danger is in the collateral requirements that can force selling at the worst time

On the high-yield credit default swaps:
> "We insure against natural hurricanes. And we insure against a financial hurricane. And we have been in a bit of a financial hurricane."

### Financial Literacy

Buffett discussed the importance of financial education, noting the problem exists in current generations, not just future ones.

### Board of Directors

Howard Buffett, Susan Decker, Bill Gates, Sandy Gottesman, Charlotte Guyman, Don Keough, Tom Murphy, Ron Olson, and Walter Scott.
