# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 1987

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 1987

## Overview

- **Attendance**: ~500 shareholders at Joslyn Art Museum
- **Notable**: Buffett consuming Cherry Coke during Q&A (per Omaha World-Herald)

## Key Topics

### No Stock Split

Buffett humorously refused splitting:
> "I have a stockholder friend of mine who is 60. I just sent him a telegram on his birthday that said, 'May you live until Berkshire Hathaway splits.'"

The implication: Berkshire would never split. The stock was already trading above $3,000.

> "Berkshire already has the exact group of patient, long-term shareholders that he wants — and it's 'a hard group to upgrade.'"

### NYSE vs OTC

At the time, Berkshire traded over-the-counter. Buffett preferred it that way:
> "The less stock traded, the better job we've done. That's not an attitude the NYSE likes."

NYSE wanted Berkshire to list but wouldn't accept 10-share round lots (Buffett's proposed compromise). Berkshire wanted 10-share lots because 100-share lots would be prohibitively expensive at Berkshire's price.

> "The New York Stock Exchange wants us, but not the 10-share round lot."

The NYSE agreed to Berkshire's terms the following year, and Berkshire joined the Big Board on November 29, 1988 — with 10-share round lots.
