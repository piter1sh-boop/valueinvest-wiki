# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting 1986

**Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger** | 1986

## Overview

- **Attendance**: ~500 shareholders at Joslyn Art Museum
- **Venue**: Witherspoon Concert Hall, Omaha

## Key Topics

### Inflation Outlook

Buffett warned about future inflation:
> "It is a political phenomenon, not an economic one. If you print lots of tickets, ticket prices decline. If you print lots of dollars, the value of each dollar goes down. Eventually, that has to happen."

> "I expect a lot of inflation in the long run. Unfortunately, I can't define 'a lot' and I can't define 'the long run.'"

> "Most people in this room will see inflation that they have never seen before."

### Acquisition Philosophy

Buffett compared acquisitions to selecting a wife:
> "We have no equations. Ben Graham used to say that it's a lot like selecting a wife. You can thoughtfully establish certain qualities you'd like her to have — and then, all of a sudden, you meet someone and you do it."

On capital for acquisitions:
> "Don't hold back on showing us a [potential] deal because we don't have the money. We have yet to not do a deal because of not having enough money."

### Capital Cities/ABC Investment

Buffett on the 1985 investment:
> "Capital Cities is not a stock holding, but a 10-20 year relationship."

> "They have strong management at Cap Cities — the best management of any public company in the country. I have enormous confidence in Cap Cities."

On ABC's future:
> "The networks did $6.5 billion of business last year. It will be there — like radio. Despite inroads from television, [radio] is still quite profitable. ABC will be around forever."

On selling Cap Cities earlier (hinting at regret):
> "I have repressed the memory of the sale of Capital Cities [a few years earlier]."
