# Charlie Munger

**Charles Thomas Munger** (January 1, 1924 – November 28, 2023) was vice chairman of Berkshire Hathaway and Warren Buffett's partner for over six decades. Known as the "architect" of Berkshire's modern investment approach, Munger brought a multidisciplinary framework to investing that influenced Buffett's evolution from "cigar-butt" investing to buying "wonderful businesses at fair prices."

## The Munger Framework

Munger's genius lay in synthesizing knowledge from multiple disciplines—what he called the **"latticework of mental models"**:

> "You have to know the big ideas in all the major disciplines—all the really important ones. And you have to probably know them well enough that you can use them in combination."

### Key Mental Models

#### Psychology
Munger was deeply interested in **human psychology and behavioral biases**. His talk *"The Psychology of Human Misjudgment"* (1995) outlined 25 causes of human error:

- **Liking/Loving**: We overvalue things we like
- **Disliking/Hating**: We undervalue things we oppose
- **Doubt-Avoidance**: We tend to decide quickly and then rationalize
- **Incentive/Super-Incentive**: What gets rewarded gets done
- **Social Proof**: We follow what others do

#### Economics
- **Principal-Agent Problem**: Managers may act in self-interest rather than shareholder interest
- **Mercantile Advantage**: Scale advantages in business (network effects, cost advantages)
- **Compounding**: The eighth wonder of the world

#### Engineering
- **Breakpoints/Redundancy**: Systems should have margin of safety at failure points
- **Quality/Price Relationship**: Quality often has a better price-value ratio than cheap alternatives

## Investment Approach

### "Wonderful Business at Fair Price"
Munger convinced Buffett to shift from Graham's "cheap cigar-butt" approach to buying **high-quality businesses** with durable competitive advantages:

> "The difference between a good business and a bad business is that a good business gives you a lot of money to take out. A bad one gives you a lot of problems to solve."

### traits Munger Sought

1. **Large moat** around the business
2. **Honest, competent management**
3. **Simple businesses** he could understand
4. **Purchase at reasonable price**, not cheap price

## Munger's Portfolio (Poor Charlie's Almanack)

### Coca-Cola
Munger recommended buying Coca-Cola in 1988 when it traded at a high multiple. His reasoning: the moat was so wide (brand, distribution, pricing power) that paying a "fair" price was better than waiting for a "cheap" price that might never come.

### Daily Journal
Munger ran Daily Journal Corporation's investment portfolio with similar principles. The portfolio held primarily equities and Treasury bonds, with a focus on "right businesses" rather than speculative opportunities.

## Famous Quotes

- "Spend each day trying to be a little wiser than you were when you woke up."
- "All the advantage goes to the person who can be right on the few really important things and stay with it."
- "Taking a different view of human nature is a prerequisite to investment success."
- "The highest return you can get is to become more rational."
- "A lot of people have trouble with the idea of a value approach to investing because life is not set up to reward those who are patient."

## Legacy

Munger's greatest contribution was teaching Buffett—and the world—that:

1. **Right business matters more than right price**
2. **Multidisciplinary thinking prevents catastrophic errors**
3. **Psychology is central to understanding markets and management**
