# Warren Buffett

**Warren Edward Buffett** (born August 30, 1930) is an American investor, business tycoon, and philanthropist. He is widely regarded as one of the most successful investors in history. As the chairman and CEO of **Berkshire Hathaway**, he has accumulated a personal net worth exceeding $100 billion.

## Investment Philosophy

Buffett's investment approach centers on **value investing** principles taught by Benjamin Graham:

- **Margin of Safety**: Only buy when the price is significantly below intrinsic value
- **Circle of Competence**: Invest only in businesses you understand
- **Moat**: Seek companies with durable competitive advantages
- **Long-term Thinking**: Best returns come from holding quality businesses for decades

## Key Principles

### 1. Intrinsic Value
> "Intrinsic value is the discounted value of the cash that can be taken out of a business during its remaining life."

Buffett calculates intrinsic value using **owner earnings** rather than GAAP earnings, factoring in depreciation and capital expenditures.

### 2. Moat (Economic Castle)
> "The key to investing is not assessing how much an industry is going to affect society, or how much it will grow, but rather determining the competitive advantage of any given company, and above all, the durability of that advantage."

The moat is the competitive advantage that protects a business from competitors—a brand, patent, network effect, or cost advantage that cannot be easily replicated.

### 3. Mr. Market
> "Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful."

Derived from Graham's allegory, Mr. Market represents the daily fluctuations of stock prices. Buffett uses these irrational movements as opportunities to buy when prices are depressed.

## Berkshire Hathaway

Founded in 1839 as a textile company, Berkshire became Buffett's investment vehicle after he acquired it in the 1960s. Today it operates **two major businesses**:

1. **Insurance Operations** — Including GEICO, the sixth-largest auto insurer in the US, and reinsurance giant General Re
2. **Wholly-Owned Businesses** — BNSF Railway, MidAmerican Energy, See's Candies, and 70+ other subsidiaries

The **float** (premiums collected but not yet paid out) from insurance operations has been Berkshire's "weapon" — cheap capital deployed into investments.
