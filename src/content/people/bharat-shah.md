# Bharat Shah

## Overview

Bharat Shah is an Indian value investor and author. He wrote "Observations, Ideas and Reflections on Long-term Value and Wealth Creation from Equity Investing," a decade-long study distilling investment principles validated in the Indian market context.

## Key Philosophy

### Art and Science of Investing

Bharat Shah emphasizes that investing is a blend of art and science:

> "Good investing is a heady mix of both a lovely, beauteous art and a very precise looking science."

The **art part** involves:
- Understanding business quality and character
- Appreciating what makes businesses work or fail
- Developing wisdom, discipline, patience, and independent thinking

The **science part** involves:
- Transcribing intangible qualities into tangible analysis
- Building working models including mathematical frameworks

### Value vs. Price

Shah emphasizes the contrast between price and value:

> "Price is certain and upfront. Value is a conjecture (albeit an intelligent one) and will unravel only over time in future."

### Key Principles

1. **Margin of Safety** — Purchase price should be set at a good discount to conjectured value
2. **Long-term Focus** — "Equity investing is indeed long-term even though it is not considered fashionable these days"
3. **Quality of Returns** — Focus on certainty and quality of growth, not just quantum
4. **Discipline** — Follow absolute returns, not relative returns
5. **Patience** — "The difficult part is getting the right psychological traits, a disciplined behavioral make-up, a quiet sense of innate independent, curious mind"

### On Risk

> "Returns are tangible but the risk is not. Hence, the returns appear as real to the investors but risk appears as amorphous."

He distinguishes between:
- Certainty of growth vs. quantum of growth
- Quantum of growth vs. quality of growth
- Quality of growth vs. valuation
