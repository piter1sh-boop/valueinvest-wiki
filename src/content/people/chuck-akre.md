# Chuck Akre

## Overview

Chuck Akre is the founder and chief investment officer of Akre Capital Management. He is a renowned value investor known for his focus on businesses with exceptional reinvestment opportunities and his "three-legged stool" investment philosophy.

## Investment Philosophy

### The Three Legs of the Investment Stool

Akre's investment approach rests on three essential pillars:

1. **Extraordinary Business Model** — A business with sustainable competitive advantages
2. **Exceptional People** — Management with integrity and capital allocation skills
3. **Abundant Reinvestment Opportunities** — The ability to grow capital at high rates

### Focus on Reinvestment

Akre emphasizes that the ability to reinvest capital at high rates of return is the key driver of long-term value creation:

> "The ability to earn earnings upon earnings is essentially the definition of compounding. In the long run, we feel strongly that the rate at which the value of a business compounds will approximate its returns on reinvestment."

### Dividend Reality

> "Dividends are the route to average returns, and our goal is decidedly to seek above-average returns. Excellence in reinvestment is the route to such returns."

He illustrates this with a mathematical example:
- A business with 21% return on capital
- If purchased at 3x book value
- A 100% dividend payout yields only 7% return (21%/3 = 7%)
- This is average performance despite excellent underlying business

### Key Insight

> "The reality is that the price a shareholder pays for a wonderful business is typically a substantial multiple of the actual capital invested in that business."

## The Compounding Machine

Even ordinary businesses can become extraordinary compounding machines with outstanding reinvestors. Berkshire Hathaway is the prime example — beginning as a struggling textile mill and compounding at extraordinary rates under Warren Buffett's capital allocation.

## Reinvestment Strategies

Akre observes different reinvestment strategies among portfolio companies:

- **Acquisition-focused** — Companies expert at making bolt-on acquisitions
- **Organic reinvestment** — Fixed assets and inventory purchases to expand operations
- **Opportunistic allocation** — Across multiple asset classes and industries

One company described it as: "Each day, we get to choose from a varied menu as to how to allocate capital to continue to build the value of your company."
