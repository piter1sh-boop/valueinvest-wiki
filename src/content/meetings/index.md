# Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meetings

The **Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting** (known as "Woodstock for Capitalists") is the most famous shareholder meeting in the world. Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger (until 2023) field questions from shareholders for hours, sharing their investment wisdom, business insights, and timeless lessons.

> "The annual meeting is where you get to see Charlie and me operate. We don't rehearse. What you see is what you get."
> — Warren Buffett

## Key Themes Across Meetings

### On Intrinsic Value
Buffett consistently explains intrinsic value calculation, emphasizing "owner earnings" over GAAP earnings.

### On Mr. Market
The [[mr-market|Mr. Market]] concept is demonstrated live as Buffett and Munger react to hypothetical scenarios.

### On Capital Allocation
Buffett details [[capital-allocation|capital allocation]] decisions — when to invest, buy back stock, or hold cash.

### On Special Situations
Many meetings contain discussions of [[special-situations]] investing, spin-offs, and arbitrage opportunities.

### On Succession
The question of succession has been asked for decades. See [[buffett-succession]] for the evolution of this topic.

## Famous Meeting Moments

### 2020 Virtual Meeting
First-ever virtual annual meeting due to COVID. Buffett and Munger (via Zoom) conducted the meeting from separate locations.

### 2019 Final Munger Meeting
The last in-person meeting with Charlie Munger before his passing in 2023.

### 2008 Financial Crisis
Buffett was actively buying during the panic and explained his reasoning in real-time.

### 1999 Internet Bubble
Munger's famous warnings about "human cleverness gone wrong" at the peak of the bubble.
