# 2002 Annual Meeting

**Date:** May 4, 2002
**Location:** Omaha, Nebraska
**Attendees:** Warren Buffett (71), Charlie Munger (78)
**Source:** Full Transcript

## Overview

The 2002 annual meeting was held after September 11, 2001. Buffett discussed terrorism insurance, Berkshire's resilience, and America's determination to recover from the attacks.

## Opening

Buffett on America:
> "America was attacked. But we're not broken."

On resilience:
> "We'll bounce back. We always do."

## Business Results

On the insurance impact:
> "9/11 changed everything. Insurance costs rose."

On Berkshire:
> "We paid claims promptly. That's what we do."

## Key Topics

### Terrorism Insurance

On the new risks:
> "Terrorism is uninsurable in the old sense. The risks are systemic."

On government involvement:
> "The government needs to backstop terrorism insurance. Private markets can't handle it alone."

On pricing:
> "Rates have risen. That's appropriate given the risks."

### Post-9/11 World

On the attack's impact:
> "Everything changed after 9/11. We can't pretend otherwise."

On being prepared:
> "We can't prevent terrorism. But we can insure against it properly."

### Berkshire's Strength

On capital:
> "We have the capital to pay any claim. That's our promise."

On reputation:
> "Our word is good. We pay what we owe."

### America's Determination

On recovery:
> "America is determined. We'll rebuild and move forward."

On staying invested:
> "Don't panic. Stay the course."

## Notable Quotes

> "America was attacked. But we're not broken."

> "We'll bounce back. We always do."

> "Terrorism is uninsurable in the old sense."

## Key Themes

1. **9/11** - The changed world
2. **Terrorism Insurance** - New risks
3. **Resilience** - Berkshire's strength
4. **Recovery** - America's determination
5. **Long-Term** - Don't panic
