## 文章 1: 巴菲特语录系列

**适合发布在**: 知乎专栏、雪球

**标题**: 巴菲特最经典的20句投资箴言（中英对照）

**正文**:

巴菲特的投资智慧历经60年验证，每一句都值得反复研读。以下精选20句经典语录，附上英文原文和简要解读：

1. **"Rule No. 1: Never lose money. Rule No. 2: Never forget Rule No. 1."**
   投资的第一原则是不要亏损，第二原则是永远不要忘记第一原则。

2. **"Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful."**
   别人恐惧时我贪婪，别人贪婪时我恐惧。

3. **"It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price."**
   以合理价格买一家优秀公司，远比以优惠价格买一家普通公司要好。

4. **"The key to investing is not assessing how much an industry is going to affect society, or how much it will grow, but rather determining the competitive advantage of any given company, and above all, the durability of that advantage."**
   投资的秘诀不在于评估某个行业对社会的影响或增长前景，而在于确定任意公司的竞争优势，最重要的是其优势的持久性。

5. **"Price is what you pay. Value is what you get."**
   价格是你付出的，价值是你得到的。

6. **"In the short run, the market is a voting machine. In the long run, it is a weighing machine."**
   短期来看，市场是个投票机；长期来看，市场是个称重机。

7. **"Risk comes from not knowing what you are doing."**
   风险来自于不知道自己在做什么。

8. **"Diversification is protection against ignorance."**
   分散化是对无知的保护。

9. **"The most important investment you can make is in yourself."**
   最重要的投资是对自己的投资。

10. **"Someone is sitting in the shade today because someone planted a tree a long time ago."**
    今天坐在阴凉处的人，是因为很久以前种了一棵树。

11. **"Buffett's favorite holding period is forever."**
    巴菲特最喜欢的持有期限是永远。

12. **"Only buy something that you'd be perfectly happy to hold if the market shut down for 10 years."**
    只买那些即使市场关闭10年你也愿意持有的股票。

13. **"You don't need to be a rocket scientist. Investing is not a game where the guy with the 160 IQ wins over the guy with the 130 IQ."**
    你不需要是火箭科学家。投资不是智商160的人击败智商130的人的游戏。

14. **"The business schools reward complex behavior more than simple behavior, but simple behavior works better."**
    商学院奖励复杂行为而非简单行为，但简单行为效果更好。

15. **"Honesty is a very expensive gift. Do not expect it from cheap people."**
    诚实是一种非常昂贵的礼物，不要期望从廉价的人那里得到它。

16. **"I will tell you how to become rich. Close the doors. Be fearful when others are greedy and greedy when others are fearful."**
    我告诉你如何致富。关上门。当别人恐惧时贪婪，当别人贪婪时恐惧。

17. **"The difference between successful people and really successful people is that really successful people say no to almost everything."**
    成功人士和真正成功人士的区别在于，真正成功的人对几乎所有事情都说"不"。

18. **"Your premium for a business is determined by the odds that you are certain about and the uncertainty of everything else."**
    你为一个企业支付的溢价取决于你所确定的几率以及其他一切的不确定性。

19. **"Accounting is the language of business."**
    会计是商业的语言。

20. **"When we own portions of outstanding businesses with outstanding managements, our favorite holding period is forever."**
    当我们拥有优秀企业和优秀管理层的一部分时，我们最喜欢的持有期限是永远。

## 文章 2: 护城河概念解析

**适合发布在**: 知乎专栏、雪球

**标题**: 什么是"护城河"？巴菲特选股的核心标准

**正文**:

巴菲特曾说："投资的秘诀在于确定任意公司的竞争优势，最重要的是其优势的持久性。"这种竞争优势，就是"经济护城河"。

### 什么是经济护城河？

经济护城河（Economic Moat）指企业抵御竞争对手的能力，如同 medieval castle 的护城河保护城堡。一个拥有宽阔护城河的公司，能够在数十年内保持超常盈利能力。

### 护城河的四种类型

**1. 无形资产**
品牌、专利、监管许可证等竞争对手难以复制的资产。
- 可口可乐：品牌价值超过700亿美元
- 苹果：品牌 + iOS生态系统锁定效应

**2. 成本优势**
能够以低于竞争对手的成本生产或交付。
- BNSF铁路：每吨英里运输成本是卡车的1/3
- Costco：规模经济实现天天低价

**3. 转换成本**
客户转向竞争对手需要付出的金钱、心理或时间成本。
- Microsoft Office：学习曲线陡峭
- 企业软件（SAP、Oracle）：多年培训投入

**4. 网络效应**
产品随用户增多而更有价值。
- Visa/Mastercard：更多商户→更多用户→更多商户
- Facebook：更多用户→更高广告价值

### 为什么护城河如此重要？

芒格帮助巴菲特意识到：以合理价格买一家优秀公司，比以优惠价格买一家普通公司要好。原因是：拥有宽阔护城河的企业可以数十年的高复合增长率复利增长，而普通企业无论多便宜，其回报最终都会被竞争侵蚀。

## 文章 3: 芒格智慧系列

**适合发布在**: 知乎专栏、雪球

**标题**: 查理·芒格的15条人生智慧

**正文**:

查理·芒格不仅是巴菲特的搭档，更是20世纪最杰出的思想家之一。以下是他最值得收藏的15条智慧：

1. **"All intelligent life is learning survival."**
   所有智慧的生命都在学习生存。

2. **"I regard an ability to avoid idiocy as more important than intelligence."**
   我认为避免愚蠢的能力比智力更重要。

3. **"The higher your standards, the fewer mistakes you make."**
   你的标准越高，犯的错误就越少。

4. **"Spend each day trying to be a little wiser than you were when you woke up."**
   每天努力比醒来时聪明一点。

5. **"The person who is the best at mitigating the downside tends to also have the biggest upside."**
   最擅长控制下行风险的人往往也有最大的上行空间。

6. **"It's not the pure white snow that we need, it's the reliable process that produces the right answer most of the time."**
   我们需要的不是纯粹的白雪，而是能在大多数时候产生正确答案的可靠过程。

7. **"If you don't mind being wrong, you can try anything. But you won't learn from it."**
   如果你不介意犯错，你可以尝试任何事情。但你不会从中学习。

8. **"The的痛苦的一个重要来源是，我们对自己、对世界、对未来都有很多错误的看法。"**
   （原文："A big source of misery is having a lot of wrong views about ourselves, the world, the future."）

9. **"The best advice I can give you is to be prepared. Being prepared gives you more options and more chances."**
   我能给你的最好建议是做好准备。做好准备给你更多的选择和机会。

10. **"Every person is going to have a different portfolio, and that portfolio should fit your own temperament."**
    每个人的投资组合都不同，那个投资组合应该适合你自己的性格。

## Medium 文章

**标题**: How Buffett's $150 Billion Moat Built Berkshire Hathaway

**正文**:

Warren Buffett transformed Berkshire Hathaway from a dying textile company into a $900 billion conglomerate. Here's how he did it—and the investing principles you can learn from.

### The Moat Strategy

Buffett's core insight: **Buy wonderful businesses at fair prices, not fair businesses at wonderful prices.**

The "moat" concept is simple—find businesses with sustainable competitive advantages that keep competitors at bay for decades.

### Berkshire's Four Horses

By 2024, Berkshire operates with four distinct "horses":

1. **Insurance** (GEICO, General Re) - Float provides cheap capital
2. **Railroad** (BNSF) - Cost advantage over trucking
3. **Energy** (Berkshire Hathaway Energy) - Regulated monopolies
4. **Manufacturing/Retail** (30+ subsidiaries) - Diversified businesses

### The Float Engine

The insurance float—premiums collected but not yet paid out—is Berkshire's secret weapon. At $170+ billion, this "free" capital compounds in Buffett's hands at rates impossible for most investors.

### Key Takeaway

Buffett's wealth wasn't built on picking stocks. It was built on **buying wonderful businesses and letting them compound for decades**.
